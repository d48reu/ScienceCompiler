Butyrate / Neuroinflammation Executive Brief

Bottom line
In the current gold corpus, direct sodium butyrate mostly looks anti-neuroinflammatory across multiple non-parkinsonian preclinical contexts, but one parkinsonian MPTP study points the other way. The best current interpretation is not “butyrate works” or “butyrate fails,” but that its neuroimmune effects are context-dependent.

What we analyzed
- 17 study summaries in the broader corpus
- gold-set curation down to 14 higher-value claims
- contradiction detection, ontology normalization, evidence weighting, and disease-family splitting

Most important result
For the core direct-intervention bucket:
- sodium butyrate -> neuroinflammation
- overall gold-set net support: positive

But family-split views are more informative:
- parkinsonian: negative signal
- alcohol-related: positive signal
- alzheimer-related: positive signal
- acute injury: positive signal
- gut-inflammation spillover: positive signal
- toxic exposure: positive signal

What this means
The key scientific object is the sign flip. Sodium butyrate is not behaving like a universally protective anti-inflammatory intervention. The current corpus suggests that disease program and model context matter enough to reverse the apparent direction of effect.

Why this is interesting
If true, this moves the question from “is butyrate anti-inflammatory?” to “under what disease programs does butyrate help versus harm?” That is a much more valuable scientific question.

Strongest negative anchor
- PMID 32556930
- MPTP Parkinson model
- sodium butyrate worsened neuroinflammation and broader disease pathology

Strongest positive anchors

Current limitations
- abstract/summarized text only, not full-paper adjudication
- heuristic evidence weighting, not formal meta-analysis
- some buckets still single-paper
- reviewed corpus is curated but still not publication-grade evidence synthesis

Best next move
Deep-review the core contradiction cluster and build a structured contrast table around:
- disease/model family
- route
- dose
- timing
- barrier state
- microbiome state
- inflammatory readouts

Decision-grade takeaway
The project has moved beyond a toy. The current best claim is:
Direct sodium butyrate appears predominantly anti-neuroinflammatory across several non-parkinsonian preclinical contexts, but a parkinsonian MPTP model shows the opposite direction, suggesting disease-context-dependent neuroimmune effects.
