Hackathon demo script

Target length:
60 to 90 seconds.

Open:
"Most AI-for-science tools summarize papers or predict properties. We built something different: a Science Compiler. It turns literature into structured claims, contradictions, weighted evidence, and experiment ideas."

Show the butyrate corpus first.

Step 1:
Show the corpus folder.
Say:
"Here are raw study summaries in one narrow scientific question: when does butyrate reduce neuroinflammation, and when might it worsen it?"

Suggested terminal view:
/home/d48reu/science-compiler/corpora/butyrate_neuroinflammation/

Step 2:
Run or show the gold report.
Say:
"The compiler extracts and refines claims, canonicalizes labels, weights evidence, and splits the literature by disease family instead of flattening everything into one average answer."

Best file to show:
/home/d48reu/science-compiler/corpora/butyrate_neuroinflammation/reports/compiler_report_gold.md

Step 3:
Show the key result.
Say:
"It finds that direct sodium butyrate looks mostly anti-neuroinflammatory across alcohol-related, Alzheimer-related, acute-injury, gut-inflammation, and toxic-exposure contexts — but a Parkinson/MPTP paper points the other way."

Best lines to show:
- direct butyrate | parkinsonian | neuroinflammation
- direct butyrate | alcohol_related | neuroinflammation
- direct butyrate | alzheimer_related | neuroinflammation

Step 4:
Show that it is not just one-off.
Say:
"Then we ran a second domain: senolytics and aging. There the structure is different — not one clean contradiction, but a benefit-versus-harm tradeoff map across agents like D+Q, fisetin, and navitoclax."

Best file to show:
/home/d48reu/science-compiler/corpora/senolytics_aging/reports/TRADEOFF_MEMO.md

Step 5:
Close with the core claim.
Say:
"So the point is not that the AI is replacing scientists. The point is that it gives researchers a new way to compute over literature: find the real disagreements, preserve nuance, and generate sharper next experiments."

Final line:
"Science Compiler turns papers from things you read into things you can compute over."
