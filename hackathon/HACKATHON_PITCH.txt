Science Compiler

One-line pitch:
An AI system that compiles messy scientific literature into structured claims, contradiction maps, weighted evidence balances, and decisive experiment ideas.

Why this fits the hackathon:
- Creativity: it is not another chatbot or RAG wrapper; it treats papers as inputs to a compiler pipeline.
- Usefulness: it helps researchers find where the literature actually disagrees instead of averaging everything into mush.
- Presentation: the demo is legible, visual, and terminal-friendly. Raw papers go in, structured scientific tension comes out.

What to emphasize:
1. This is AI as a scientific instrument, not AI as the subject.
2. It finds context-dependent contradictions instead of flattening them.
3. It supports human review instead of pretending to be fully autonomous.
4. It already works across two domains:
   - butyrate / neuroinflammation
   - senolytics / aging

Best butyrate story:
- direct sodium butyrate looks mostly anti-neuroinflammatory across several non-parkinsonian contexts
- but one MPTP Parkinson paper flips the sign
- the system surfaces that contradiction and proposes moderator-focused follow-up experiments

Best senolytics story:
- the system generalizes to a different domain
- instead of one core contradiction, it reveals benefit-vs-harm tradeoffs across senolytic agents
- that shows the compiler is not overfit to one pattern

What NOT to claim:
- do not say it proves new biology
- do not say it replaces experts
- do not say it is a full paper-reading scientist
- do say it is a new workflow for turning literature into structured scientific disagreement and better hypotheses

Best framing sentence:
Science Compiler turns papers from things you read into things you can compute over.
