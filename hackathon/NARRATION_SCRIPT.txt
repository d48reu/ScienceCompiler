Most AI for science tools summarize papers or predict on datasets. We built something different: Science Compiler. It turns scientific literature into structured claims, contradiction maps, weighted evidence, and experiment ideas.

In our first domain, butyrate and neuroinflammation, the system found a real context-dependent sign flip. Direct sodium butyrate looks mostly anti-neuroinflammatory across several non-Parkinsonian contexts, but one M. P. T. P. Parkinson paper points the other way.

In our second domain, senolytics and aging, the structure changes. Instead of one central contradiction, the system surfaces a benefit-versus-harm tradeoff map across agents like dasatinib plus quercetin, fisetin, and navitoclax.

So the point is not that AI replaces scientists. The point is that it gives researchers a new way to compute over literature, preserve disagreement, and generate sharper next experiments.

Science Compiler turns papers from things you read into things you can compute over.
