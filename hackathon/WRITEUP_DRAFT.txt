Science Compiler

What it is
Science Compiler is a local-first AI workflow for turning scientific literature into structured, computable objects instead of prose summaries.

Given a small corpus of papers or study summaries, it extracts claims, refines them, canonicalizes interventions and outcomes, weights evidence, detects contradictions, groups mechanisms, and proposes follow-up experiments.

Why we built it
Most AI-for-science tools either summarize literature or predict on datasets. We wanted to build something that helps with a different bottleneck: holding contradictory scientific evidence in one structure and making that disagreement legible.

How it works
- raw study text in
- first-pass claim extraction
- second-pass refinement
- curation and ranking
- ontology / canonicalization
- evidence weighting
- contradiction detection
- family-split evidence balance
- review queue for human correction

What it found
1. Butyrate / neuroinflammation
The compiler found that direct sodium butyrate mostly looks anti-neuroinflammatory across several non-parkinsonian contexts, but a parkinsonian MPTP paper points the other way. The useful scientific output is not a universal claim, but a sharper question: why does the sign flip in that disease context?

2. Senolytics / aging
In a second domain, the system surfaced a different structure: not one clean contradiction, but a benefit-vs-harm tradeoff map. Some senolytics improve lifespan, function, or resilience, while some agents or contexts show harms, null effects, or sex-specific downside.

Why this matters
The project is trying to make AI useful as a scientific instrument rather than a generic assistant. Instead of replacing expert judgment, it helps researchers see where the literature is coherent, where it breaks, and what should be tested next.

Current limitations
- abstract/summarized text, not full-paper adjudication
- heuristic evidence weighting
- still needs human review for best results
- not a replacement for systematic review or meta-analysis

Why it fits the hackathon
It is creative, useful, and demoable:
- creative because it treats science like something you can compile
- useful because it surfaces contradictions instead of erasing them
- demoable because the transformation from raw papers to structured scientific tension is visible and easy to follow

One-line summary
Science Compiler turns papers from things you read into things you can compute over.
